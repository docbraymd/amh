Decoding Your Immune System

A Comprehensive Guide to Autoimmunity Testing & Root Cause Solutions

by Christopher L. Bray MD PhD

In the landscape of modern medicine, a silent revolution is underway—one that shifts the locus of control from the institution to the individual. At Archangel Michael Health, we believe that each patient must serve as the CEO of their own health. Autoimmunity is rarely a sudden event; it is a spectrum that develops over years. Conventional medicine often waits for the "scream"—when organ damage is visible. We prefer to listen to the "whispers"—subtle shifts in blood markers that offer a window of opportunity. This guide is designed to help you understand the specific blood tests we use, when they are appropriate, and how to intervene early using common-sense steps before expensive medications become necessary.

1. The "Why" and "When" of Testing

Important Clinical Context

It is critical to understand that autoimmune tests are not screening tests for the general healthy population. A positive result does not automatically mean you have a disease.

Up to 30% of healthy individuals (especially women and the elderly) may have low-level positive antibodies without ever developing an autoimmune condition. Ordering these tests without specific symptoms often leads to "rabbit holes" of anxiety and unnecessary medical workups.

When do we order these tests? We investigate when there is a "cluster" of clinical signals, such as:

  • Joint Symptoms: Swelling, redness, or stiffness that lasts more than an hour in the morning (inflammatory arthritis vs. wear-and-tear).
  • Skin Changes: Rashes that are photosensitive (worse with sun), butterfly-shaped across the cheeks, or palpable purpura (spots).
  • Constitutional Symptoms: Profound, unexplained fatigue, low-grade fevers, or unintended weight loss.
  • Organ Involvement: Unexplained kidney issues (protein in urine), dry eyes/mouth (sicca), or nerve pain/numbness.

2. The Barometers: ESR & CRP

Before identifying what is being attacked, we must establish if there is a systemic fire and how hot it is burning. These are the two primary sentinels of inflammation.

ESR (Sed Rate)
CHRONIC MARKER
What it is
The Erythrocyte Sedimentation Rate measures how fast red blood cells fall in a tube. Inflammation creates proteins (fibrinogen) that make cells sticky, causing them to clump (rouleaux) and fall faster.
When to Order
To monitor known chronic conditions (like Lupus or Polymyalgia Rheumatica) over weeks or months. It is a "lagging indicator"—it rises slowly and falls slowly.
Interpretation
Optimal: <10 mm/hr (men), <15 mm/hr (women).
High: >40 mm/hr suggests significant inflammation.
Nuance: Obesity increases ESR because fat cells release inflammatory cytokines. A moderately high ESR in an overweight patient without joint pain is often metabolic, not autoimmune.
hs-CRP
ACUTE / SILENT MARKER
What it is
C-Reactive Protein is produced by the liver in direct response to immune signaling (IL-6). It rises within hours of an insult.
When to Order
To detect acute flares, infection, or "silent" inflammation linked to heart disease.
Interpretation
Optimal: <0.5 mg/L (Cool system).
Warning: 1.0–3.0 mg/L (Leaky gut, insulin resistance, or early reactivity).
Danger: >3.0 mg/L (High risk for cardiovascular events; active inflammation).

3. The Gateway: ANA Testing

The Antinuclear Antibody (ANA) test is the entry point. It detects antibodies targeting the command center (nucleus) of your cells.

Warning: The "False Positive" Trap

We do NOT order ANA just for "fatigue" alone. Up to 15-30% of healthy people have a low-positive ANA. Infections (like Mono/EBV) and medications can also trigger a positive result.

Interpreting ANA
DIAGNOSTICS
The Titer (Quantity)
1:40 or 1:80: "Low Positive." Often seen in healthy aging or viral infections. Rarely indicates active autoimmune disease. We usually monitor these with lifestyle changes.
1:160: The cutoff for clinical significance.
1:320 or higher: "High Positive." Strongly suggests an underlying autoimmune process (Lupus, Scleroderma, etc.).
The Pattern (Target)
Homogeneous: The whole nucleus glows. Classic for Lupus (SLE).
Speckled: "Starry night" look. Non-specific; seen in Sjogren's, Lupus, or general environmental reactivity.
Centromere: Highly specific for Limited Scleroderma (CREST syndrome).

4. Specific Disease Antibodies

If the ANA is positive and symptoms align, we "reflex" to specific tests to identify the exact fingerprint of the immune attack.

Lupus (SLE) & Connective Tissue
SPECIFIC MARKERS
Anti-dsDNA
The Tracker. Highly specific (97%) for Lupus. Unlike other markers, it fluctuates with disease activity. Rising levels often predict a kidney flare (Nephritis).
Anti-Sm (Smith)
The Confirm-er. The most specific antibody for Lupus. If positive, it is virtually diagnostic, though only 30% of patients have it.
Complement (C3/C4)
Low levels: Suggest active "consumption" by immune complexes (Lupus flare).
High levels: Suggest insulin resistance and metabolic syndrome (fat cells produce C3).
Rheumatoid Arthritis (RA)
JOINT HEALTH
Rheumatoid Factor (RF)
The Old Guard. An antibody that attacks your own immunoglobulins. Non-specific; can be elevated in Hep C, Sjogren's, or healthy aging. Sensitivity is only ~70%.
Anti-CCP
The Sniper. Targets "citrullinated" proteins. 96-98% specific for RA. Can appear 10-15 years before joint pain begins. Strongly linked to smoking and gum disease.

Pregnancy Warning: Anti-SSA (Ro) & Anti-SSB (La)

These antibodies are hallmarks of Sjogren's Syndrome (dry eyes/mouth) and Lupus. Clinical Criticality: Anti-SSA can cross the placenta and attack the fetal heart conduction system, causing congenital heart block. Women of childbearing age with these markers require specialized fetal monitoring during pregnancy.

5. Root Causes & Triggers

Genetics loads the gun, but environment pulls the trigger. In the "Pre-Clinical" phase (positive antibodies, mild symptoms), identifying these triggers offers the best chance to turn off the process.

  • Intestinal Permeability ("Leaky Gut"): The gut lining is regulated by a protein called Zonulin. Triggers like gluten and gut dysbiosis cause Zonulin release, opening the gates for toxins and undigested food to enter the blood. This triggers "molecular mimicry" where the immune system attacks self-tissue that resembles these invaders.
  • Viral Infections (EBV): The Epstein-Barr Virus (Mono) hides in B-cells. Its proteins structurally resemble human nuclear proteins. Chronic reactivation of EBV due to stress or poor immunity is a leading driver of ANA positivity.
  • Oral Health & The Lungs: Porphyromonas gingivalis (the bacteria of gum disease) and cigarette smoke both cause "citrullination" of proteins in the body. This is the primary driver of Anti-CCP antibodies and Rheumatoid Arthritis.
  • Environmental Toxins: Mercury (from high-predator fish or dental amalgams) and Silica dust are potent immune disruptors. Mercury levels have been shown to correlate directly with ANA titers.

6. Integrative Action Plan

Our goal is to restore tolerance. We use a "treat-to-target" approach: aggressive lifestyle and integrative support as the foundation, adding conventional medications only when necessary to prevent organ damage.

Nutritional Strategy
FOUNDATION
Mediterranean Diet
For Maintenance: The gold standard. High in Omega-3s (fish), monounsaturated fats (olive oil), and polyphenols. Proven to reduce RA pain and inflammatory markers.
Autoimmune Protocol (AIP)
For Flares/Reset: A temporary (4-12 week) elimination of grains, dairy, nightshades (tomatoes/peppers), nuts, and eggs. This removes potential immune triggers to "quiet" the system, followed by systematic reintroduction.
Gluten-Free
For Molecular Mimicry: Essential for Celiac, but also highly effective for Sjogren's and Hashimoto's due to the structural similarity between gliadin (gluten protein) and thyroid/glandular tissue.
Supplements & Herbs
INTEGRATIVE RX
Vitamin D3 + K2
Role: The "Principal" of the immune system. It teaches T-cells to distinguish self from non-self.
Target: We aim for 40-60 ng/mL (higher than standard lab range).
Cost: ~$15/month.
Omega-3 (Fish Oil)
Role: Produces "resolvins" that actively turn off inflammation pathways.
Typical Use: 2-4 grams of combined EPA/DHA daily. Ensure high quality to avoid mercury.
Cost: ~$30-40/month.
Curcumin (Turmeric)
Role: Potent anti-inflammatory that modulates cytokines (TNF-alpha). Must use bioavailable forms (with black pepper or lipid matrix).
Caution: Can act as a mild blood thinner.
Cost: ~$25/month.
L-Glutamine
Role: The primary fuel for gut lining cells (enterocytes). Helps repair "leaky gut" tight junctions.
Typical Use: 5g powder daily mixed in water.
Cost: ~$20/month.

Conventional Management

We are not anti-medication. When organs are threatened, drugs are life-saving tools.
Hydroxychloroquine (Plaquenil): Essential for Lupus to prevent permanent organ damage and reduce flares. It is generally well-tolerated but requires annual eye exams.
Goal: The minimum effective dose of medication alongside aggressive lifestyle changes to maintain remission.
