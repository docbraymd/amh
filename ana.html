Decoding Your Immune System

A Comprehensive Guide to Autoimmunity Testing & Root Cause Solutions

by Christopher L. Bray MD PhD

Autoimmunity is rarely a sudden event; it is a spectrum that develops over years. Conventional medicine often waits for the "scream"—when organ damage is visible. At Archangel Michael Health, we prefer to listen to the "whispers"—subtle shifts in blood markers that offer a window of opportunity. However, listening requires discernment. Not every ache or rash is autoimmune, and indiscriminate testing often leads to confusion rather than clarity.

On this page

1. When NOT to Order Tests

The "False Positive" Trap

It is critical to understand that autoimmune tests are not screening tests for the general healthy population. A positive result does not automatically mean you have a disease. Up to 30% of healthy individuals (especially women and the elderly) may have low-level positive antibodies without ever developing an autoimmune condition. Ordering an ANA for "general fatigue" or simple joint pain often leads to "rabbit holes" of anxiety and unnecessary medical workups.

Symptoms that mimic Autoimmunity
CLINICAL PEARLS
Osteoarthritis vs. RA
Osteoarthritis (OA): "Wear and tear." Pain gets worse with use. Stiffness <30 min. Affects finger tips (DIPs) and knees. Do NOT order ANA.
Rheumatoid Arthritis (RA): Inflammatory. Pain gets better with movement. Stiffness >1 hour. Affects knuckles (MCPs).
Rosacea vs. Lupus Rash
Rosacea: Redness on cheeks/nose with pimples. Affects the smile lines (nasolabial folds). Do NOT order ANA.
Malar Rash (Lupus): "Butterfly" shape. Photosensitive. It spares the smile lines.

2. Integrative Strategy: Osteoarthritis

If your pain is mechanical (Osteoarthritis) rather than autoimmune, ordering an ANA is not helpful. However, integrative medicine offers powerful tools beyond just Tylenol or surgery.

Osteoarthritis Protocol
NON-AUTOIMMUNE
Metabolic Weight Loss
Adipose tissue (fat) is not inert; it releases inflammatory cytokines called adipokines that actively degrade cartilage. Losing even 5-10% of body weight reduces both the mechanical load and the "chemical load" on your joints.
Movement is Medicine
Cartilage has no blood supply; it gets nutrients only through compression and release (the "sponge effect"). Sedentary behavior starves cartilage. Low-impact movement (cycling, swimming) is essential for preservation.
Curcumin (Turmeric)
Acts as a natural COX-2 inhibitor (similar to Celebrex but milder). High-quality, bioavailable curcumin can significantly reduce OA pain scores and stiffness.
Topical Therapy
Voltaren (Diclofenac) Gel: Delivers NSAID directly to the joint with minimal systemic absorption/stomach risk.
Capsaicin: Depletes "Substance P" (a pain transmitter) in the nerves over time.

3. The Barometers: ESR & CRP

ESR (Sed Rate)
CHRONIC MARKER
What it is
Measures how fast red blood cells fall. Inflammation creates fibrinogen, making cells sticky and heavy.
Nuance
Obesity increases ESR because fat cells release inflammatory cytokines. A moderately high ESR in an overweight patient without joint pain is often metabolic, not autoimmune.
hs-CRP
ACUTE / SILENT MARKER
What it is
Produced by the liver in direct response to immune signaling. Rises within hours.
Interpretation
>3.0 mg/L: Suggests active inflammation or high cardiovascular risk.

4. ANA & Reflex Patterns

If the ANA is positive, the "Pattern" and specific antibodies tell us the diagnosis. Below is a breakdown of what these markers typically signify.

Test / Pattern Associated Condition(s) Frequency / Specificity
ANA Screen General Autoimmunity Pos. in 95%+ of Lupus (SLE), but also in 15-30% of healthy individuals.
Homogeneous Pattern Systemic Lupus (SLE), Drug-Induced Lupus Common in SLE; targets DNA/Histones.
Speckled Pattern SLE, Sjogren's, MCTD, Healthy Most common non-specific pattern. Requires ENA reflex to clarify.
Centromere Pattern Limited Scleroderma (CREST) Highly specific (95%+) for CREST syndrome.
Nucleolar Pattern Scleroderma, Polymyositis Targets the nucleolus (RNA factory).
Anti-dsDNA Systemic Lupus (SLE) 95-97% Specific for SLE. Levels fluctuate with disease activity (kidney flares).
Anti-Sm (Smith) Systemic Lupus (SLE) 99% Specific for SLE. Only found in 30% of patients, but virtually diagnostic if present.
Anti-RNP Mixed Connective Tissue Disease (MCTD) Found in 95-100% of MCTD; also found in 25% of Lupus patients.
Anti-SSA (Ro) Sjogren's & Lupus 60-90% of Sjogren's; 20-60% of Lupus. Risk of neonatal heart block in pregnancy.
Anti-SSB (La) Sjogren's Syndrome Rarely appears alone; usually accompanies Anti-SSA.
Anti-Scl-70 Diffuse Systemic Sclerosis Associated with more severe skin/lung involvement in Scleroderma.
Anti-Jo-1 Polymyositis / Antisynthetase Found in ~30% of Polymyositis. Linked to "Mechanic's Hands" and lung issues.

5. Specific Disease Antibodies

Rheumatoid Arthritis (RA)
JOINT HEALTH
Rheumatoid Factor
The Old Guard. Non-specific; can be elevated in Hep C, Sjogren's, or healthy aging. Sensitivity is only ~70%.
Anti-CCP
The Sniper. 96-98% specific for RA. Can appear 10-15 years before joint pain begins. Strongly linked to smoking and gum disease.
Anti-MCV
Mutated Citrullinated Vimentin. A newer marker similar to Anti-CCP. It detects antibodies against vimentin (a structural protein). Like CCP, it is highly specific for RA and helps identify aggressive disease, sometimes catching cases that are CCP-negative.
Genetic HLA Markers
RISK PROFILE
HLA-B27
Ankylosing Spondylitis. Essential test for inflammatory back pain (worse with rest/night). A strong genetic predisposition marker.
HLA-DRB1
Rheumatoid Arthritis. The "Shared Epitope." Carriers have higher risk for RA, especially if they smoke.

6. Medications & Integrative Plan

We use a "treat-to-target" approach. While lifestyle is foundational, medications are essential when organs are threatened.

First-Line Medications
STANDARD OF CARE
Lupus (SLE)
Hydroxychloroquine (Plaquenil): The anchor drug. It prevents organ damage and flares. Generally well-tolerated but requires annual eye exams.
Rheumatoid Arthritis
Methotrexate: The gold standard "Disease Modifying" drug (DMARD). Started early to prevent joint erosion.
Biologics: Used if Methotrexate is insufficient.
Polymyalgia Rheumatica
Prednisone: PMR causes severe stiffness in shoulders/hips. It responds miraculously to low-dose prednisone (10-20mg).
Ankylosing Spondylitis
NSAIDs: High-dose NSAIDs (like Naproxen) are first-line.
TNF-Inhibitors: (e.g., Humira) highly effective for the spine.
Integrative Strategy
LIFESTYLE RX
Nutrition
Mediterranean Diet: High in Omega-3s. Proven to reduce RA pain.
Autoimmune Protocol (AIP): For active flares. Eliminates grains/dairy to "reset."
Supplements
Vitamin D: Target 40-60 ng/mL.
Omega-3s: 2-4g daily for inflammation.
Curcumin: Potent anti-inflammatory for joint pain.
Root Causes
Leaky Gut: Treat dysbiosis/gluten triggers.
Oral Health: Treat gum disease to lower RA antibodies.
